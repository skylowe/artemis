## Description
Estimates of armed forces overseas for 1940-1949, before troopdata package coverage begins. Corresponds to TR2025 Input #44 (partial).

## Sources
- National WWII Museum. "Research Starters: US Military by the Numbers." Total personnel: 1940=458K, 1941=1.8M, 1942=3.9M, 1943=9.2M, 1944=11.6M, 1945=12.2M. "73% served overseas" during 1941-1945; 7.6M overseas at peak. https://www.nationalww2museum.org/students-teachers/student-resources/research-starters/research-starters-us-military-numbers
- U.S. Army Center of Military History. "History of Personnel Demobilization in the United States Army." CMH Pub 104-8. Army declined from 8M (Aug 1945) to 3M (Jan 1946) to 554K (Mar 1948). https://www.history.army.mil/html/books/104/104-8/CMH_Pub_104-8.pdf
- Britannica. "United States Army: WWII, Korean War, and the Cold War."

## Notes
- 1940-1941: Small garrison forces (Philippines, Panama, Caribbean bases)
- 1942-1945: Active war, 60-73% of total military overseas
- 1946-1949: Occupation forces declining to pre-Korea baseline

## Format
- `year`: Calendar year
- `total_military`: Total active duty military personnel
- `overseas_troops`: Estimated troops stationed overseas
- `source`: Primary data source
